description: The Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA), established
  January 1, 2000, regulates the trucking industry in the United States. FMCSA is
  headquartered in Washington, D.C., and employs more than 1,000 people. The primary
  mission of the FMCSA is improving the safety of commercial motor vehicles (CMV)
  and truck drivers through enactment and enforcement of safety regulations. The Offices
  of Analysis, Research and Technology are three separate divisions collectively referred
  to as ART.
